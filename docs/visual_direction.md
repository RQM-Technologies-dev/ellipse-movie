# Visual Direction

## Cinematic north star

The production's blocking, camera-angle planning, shot lists, and storyboards should use a large-scale classic adventure grammar associated with Steven Spielberg as the broad directional north star: wonder, suspense, emotional clarity, kinetic discovery, and characters staged inside readable geography.

This is a directional reference for cinematic ambition and story clarity, not a request for shot-for-shot imitation.

## Blocking principles

- Stage scenes so the audience understands geography before spectacle.
- Let character movement reveal story information.
- Use foreground, midground, and background action to make the world feel alive.
- Let blocking carry emotional turns whenever possible instead of relying only on dialogue.
- Build visual cause-and-effect chains: a look leads to a movement, a movement reveals an object, an object redirects the scene.

## Camera principles

- Favor motivated camera movement: the camera should move because emotion, curiosity, danger, or discovery pulls it.
- Use reveals that preserve wonder: show the character seeing, then let the audience discover with them.
- Preserve readable geography in action, crowd movement, and fantasy spectacle.
- Let scale grow through staging: intimate face, human obstacle, environmental reveal, cosmic or architectural awe.
- Keep camera angles emotionally legible. A shot should tell us what the character feels, not only what the world looks like.

## Storyboard principles

- Storyboards should prioritize emotional continuity over isolated pretty frames.
- Each board should explain the handoff to the next board: look, gesture, movement, sound cue, environmental cue, or object reveal.
- For major fantasy sequences, board the human anchor first, then the spectacle.
- Wonder should often arrive through the character's experience of discovery.
- Suspense should come from what the audience understands just before, or just after, the character does.

## Sequence guidance

Not every scene must become a long continuous sequence. Use sequence-style chaining when the story benefits from momentum, discovery, crowd movement, pursuit, revelation, or wonder.

The Eros Market is the first explicit sequence-style scene: many different events are stitched together in one continuous movement from port arrival to Peridot and the Academy road.

## Application to Ellipse

- **Overture:** wordless, wise, allusive, emotionally mirroring the ending.
- **Tara:** intimate geography before cosmic disruption.
- **Rain of Lights:** terrifying spectacle anchored in Aaron's body and confusion.
- **Elgia:** shipboard blocking should make a small social world feel alive.
- **Eros Market:** continuous chained discovery, not montage compression.
- **Academy:** solemn blocking, controlled geometry, philosophical pressure.
- **Cottage creation:** spectacle built from Aaron's imagined home and Aura's exertion.
- **Kingdom / Garden / Portara:** epic scale, emotional clarity, father-son inevitability.
