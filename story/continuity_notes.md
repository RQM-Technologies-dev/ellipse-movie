# Continuity Notes

Running notes on details that must remain consistent across all scenes and generated assets.

## Character Continuity

### Aaron

- Teenage farm boy with lean build, sun-darkened skin, and frightening ocean-blue eyes.
- Restless and imaginative; he envies birds and wants to see beyond Tara.
- His emotional arc in Act I runs from longing, to fear, to trust, to cosmic responsibility.
- The Act I endpoint reveals that Aaron is Eternity's father.

### Aura

- Deep-cut turquoise eyes are her most important visual anchor.
- Initially appears bruised, muddy, and wounded after the rain of lights.
- Her presence can soften, persuade, or redirect people without overt force.
- Her power is tied to Aaron's imagination and emotions, but she is later clarified as her own Ousia and the Spirit of All Things.
- The same elemental color language that surrounds the rain of lights should recur around Aura's creation sequences.

### The Teacher

- Tall dark man in gray robes.
- Resists Aura's usual influence and scrutinizes her rather than becoming charmed.
- Knows Aaron's name without introduction.
- Explains Aura through Einai/Ousia, but this explanation is incomplete.

### Eternity

- Long white hair and beard, ivory cloak, brilliant ice-blue eyes, and a staff as tall as his body.
- Speaks with authority over time but with grandfatherly warmth.
- Must visually echo Aaron enough that the father-son reveal feels inevitable in retrospect.

## Location Continuity

### Tara

- Rural, natural, and modest compared with Eros and the Kingdom.
- Lake Tara, Tara River, Tara woods, and Aaron's farm should feel geographically connected.
- Tara River functions as the first threshold and must be visually distinct from the lake.

### The Elgia

- Three-masted vessel made of Nonabel wood with ivory sails.
- The silver inscription on the Captain's door must be consistent every time it appears.
- Deck, hull, spice-room sleeping quarters, foremast, and captain's quarters should remain spatially coherent.

### Eros and the Academy

- Eros is crowded, colorful, commercial, and sensory.
- The Academy is solemn, ancient, geometric, and scholarly.
- The route to the Academy uses West Eros Stables, Tritaselo Pass, thickening pines, and the triangle marker.

### The Kingdom

- The Kingdom exists in star-warmed cosmic twilight, not ordinary day or night.
- Amethyst architecture, star reflections, crystal columns, and impossible geometry must remain consistent.
- The Garden of Time and the Portara chamber are distinct locations inside the Kingdom.

## Timeline Continuity

- Aaron meets Aura at night after falling asleep at Lake Tara.
- Aaron and Aura remain at the farm for roughly two days before leaving for Eros.
- The Elgia voyage includes enough time for shipboard routine before arrival in Eros.
- Aaron and Aura ride to the Academy at night after a long day searching Eros.
- The Academy confrontation, forest creation, cosmic transit, and Kingdom arrival all happen in a compressed emotional sequence.
- Time in the Kingdom should not be treated as ordinary clock time.

## Props and Objects

- Gold leaf in Tara River.
- Aura's silver pieces.
- Silver inscription on the Captain's door: `ELGIA LORE LASANT, ENTA TY`.
- Sapphire fruit created aboard the Elgia.
- Green Academy textbook with silver triangle.
- Peridot, the Erosian Black Hair horse.
- Suspended fire created by Aura.
- Thousand-faceted gem.
- Aura-created cottage of petrified wood and amethyst.
- Eternity's staff.
- Garden plaque with the Elgia phrase.
- Pezzotaite Portara.

## Adaptation Continuity Risks

- Do not reduce Aura to only Aaron's fantasy; Act I later clarifies she is her own Ousia.
- The Teacher's explanation must be useful but incomplete.
- Keep the Elgia phrase as a motif, not a repeated slogan.
- The rain of lights must be frightening before it is understood as creative force.
- The Act I ending depends on the audience retrospectively connecting Aaron, Aura, and Eternity.
