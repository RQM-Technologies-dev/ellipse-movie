# Einai and Ousia

## Definition in Act I

The Teacher explains Aura as an **Einai**: energy without form. When an Einai comes in contact with a human, it becomes **Ousia**, the substance form of that human's imagination.

This explanation is useful but incomplete. Eternity later clarifies that Aura is not merely Aaron's projection. She is her own Ousia and the Spirit of All Things.

## Production guidance

- The Teacher's explanation should create a crisis of trust for Aaron.
- Eternity's explanation should expand the concept, not erase The Teacher's version.
- Aura should never be staged as only a hallucination or wish-fulfillment figure.
- The visual language of Ousia should combine emotional resonance with material creation.

## Key continuity line

Aura reflects Aaron's emotions and enables his imagination, but she is also autonomous and real.
